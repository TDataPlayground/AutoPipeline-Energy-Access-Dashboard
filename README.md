# AutoPipeline-Energy-Access-Dashboard
A real-time data collection and cleaning pipeline analysing electricity access across Nigerian communities, built with Google Forms, Apps Script, and Looker Studio.

AutoPipeline is a real-time data pipeline that cleans and analyzes survey data the moment it's submitted — no manual cleaning required. This project studies how electricity access affects productivity and quality of life across Nigerian communities.

Tools used: Google Forms, Google Sheets, Google Apps Script, Looker Studio.

Here are the five questions this entire project is built to answer:
One — how many hours of grid electricity do households get every day, and how does that change depending on the area they live in?
Two — how much of their monthly income do households spend on alternative energy, like generators or solar?
Three — which alternative energy sources are most common, and do richer or poorer households use different ones?
Four — does having more electricity access mean people get more productive hours in their day?
Five — does having better electricity access make people more satisfied with their quality of life?
